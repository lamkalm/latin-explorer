Classical pronunciation
📚 Classical Latin Pronunciation Rules
V
Always W — veni = "WAY-nee"
C
Always K — Cicero = "KEE-kay-roh"
G
Always hard — ager = "AH-gair"
AE
Like "eye" — alea = "AH-lay-ah"
QU
Always KW — qui = "kwee"
I (before vowel)
Y sound — iacta = "YAHK-tah"
R
Lightly trilled — tongue tap on roof of mouth
Long vowels
Held twice as long as short vowels
No silent letters
Every letter is pronounced, always
Double consonants
Both sounded — annus ≠ anus
